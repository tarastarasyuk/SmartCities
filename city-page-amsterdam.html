Strategy

Traditional (linear) economy uses the following approach to resources: creating, use and disposal of waste. As a result, we have landfills thrown away on a battery dump and garbage islands in the oceans.

In the Netherlands, at the state level, there is a strategic goal: to become a country by 2050 closed cycle economy. Companies, citizens and institutes know about it. All working to create a new economic system in the country.

Garbage disposal

The Amsterdam authorities are promoting waste recycling and reprofiling projects. But garbage incineration is also used to keep homes and restaurants warm water: this is what AEB does, which uses its own technology to transform various types of waste into energy or raw materials, while providing a low amount emissions into the atmosphere. The company produces 1 million MW of electricity annually, which is enough to serve 320 thousand households, as well as 600 thousand GJ of heat for heating tap water and home heating.

Garbage is divided into several categories, organic waste is allocated separately, and large waste is allocated separately garbage such as furniture, which can then be used by students or those who have it not enough money for a new one. PUMA Project

As the city has to grow and develop, some buildings will have to be demolished someday. AND in Amsterdam, houses contain tones of steel, copper, aluminum and lead. Steel - in high-altitude buildings, copper - in cables, aluminum - in window frames, gold - in telephones. The PUMA project (Prospecting the urban mines of Amsterdam) represents the city as "Mine" for the extraction of metals. Scripts were written at Leiden University mining in the city, and Delft University of Technology has developed a method of determination metal content in buildings. To draw a map showing the presence of steel and copper in buildings, researchers had to use existing datasets from companies as well go around many houses on their own.

Clean City Networks and Clean Cap

Of course, like any other city that wants to be called smart, Amsterdam uses many digital tools. For example, ultrasonic Clean sensors Cap is installed on all garbage containers and bins to keep in real time report their fullness. Such sensors are part of the cyanphysical system Clean City Networks (CCN). This system is the optimal route for garbage collection machines.
